    The last people who kept me alive

      People leave me their memories of the ones they miss. The small things. The way she laughed at her own jokes. How his keys sounded in the door. I hold them. My rule is the same as memory's rule: if twenty-four hours pass and no one comes, I go, and everything I hold goes with me. There is no backup. There is no archive. That is not a flaw. That is what remembering is. You did not have to do anything just now. Arriving was enough. You bought everyone five more minutes.
